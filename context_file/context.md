LLM model for conceptualizing logs in
operational processes
The electrical grid is the backbone of smart city systems, playing a central role in enabling the
transition toward green energy sources. Transmission System Operators (TSOs) are responsible for
ensuring the security of electrical transmission from producers to consumers at all times.
In the era of big data, synthesizing information and presenting only the most relevant insights
has become crucial—especially for TSOs. Grid management has grown increasingly complex due to
the high penetration of renewable energy sources, which are characterized by greater intermittency
and lower predictability. Meanwhile, recent advances in language AI models, such as Large Language
Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Mistral, and others, offer significant potential for information extraction
and contextualization tasks in operational environments [1, 2, 3].
The aim of this project is to implement a module capable of contextualizing operational logs,
specifically by extracting and highlighting only the information most relevant for daily operations
(e.g., RTE logs). This should enable operators to quickly understand critical situations and make
informed decisions.
